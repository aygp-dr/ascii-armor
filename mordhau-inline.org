* The Mordhau (Murder-Stroke)

The *Mordhau* (German for "murder-stroke" or "death-blow") is a
half-swording technique in German medieval martial arts where the
swordsman grips the blade with both hands and strikes with the pommel
and crossguard as a makeshift mace. This technique appears in several
medieval fighting manuals (Fechtbucher), including:

- *Codex Wallerstein* (c. 1470) — folio 107v shown below
- *Hans Talhoffer's Fechtbuch* (1467)
- *Gladiatoria* manuscript (c. 1430)

The technique was employed against armored opponents when cutting with
the blade was ineffective. The crossguard and pommel could deliver
concussive blows to helmeted heads or find gaps in armor at joints.

** Historical Context

The image below is from the *Codex Wallerstein* (Cod.I.6.4º.2), a
German fighting manual now held at the University of Augsburg. Folio
107v depicts two armored combatants: one delivering a Mordhau strike
while the other defends with a pollaxe or similar polearm.

The yellow surcoat with cross indicates a knight, while the opposing
figure wears more practical fighting garb. The illustration shows the
characteristic inverted grip on the blade — note how the attacker
holds the sword by its blade to use the heavy metal fittings (pommel
and crossguard) as striking surfaces.

* Self-Hosting Pattern

This file demonstrates the org-mode *named-block + babel decode*
pattern for embedding binary images in plain text. The workflow:

1. Base64-encode the image (wrapped at 76 chars for readability)
2. Store in a named =#+begin_src text= block
3. Use elisp babel block to decode to =/tmp/=
4. Link to the decoded file for inline display

This is the same structural pattern as:
- MIME =Content-Transfer-Encoding: base64=
- RFC 2397 =data:= URIs
- Classic uuencode with different alphabet and overhead

* Decode and Display

Evaluate this block with =C-c C-c= to decode the image:

#+begin_src emacs-lisp :results none
(let* ((b64 (string-trim (org-babel-ref-resolve "mordhau-b64")))
       (data (base64-decode-string b64))
       (dest "/tmp/mordhau-1.jpg"))
  (with-temp-file dest
    (set-buffer-multibyte nil)
    (insert data))
  (message "Decoded %d bytes to %s" (length data) dest)
  (org-toggle-inline-images))
#+end_src
